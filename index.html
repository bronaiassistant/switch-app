Why Switch works
The core insight
People who have more meaningful conversations are measurably happier — not just in the moment, but over time. Switch is built on that research.
🧠
Deeper questions = deeper connection
Studies show people who engage in more substantial, personal conversations report higher wellbeing and social satisfaction than those who stick to small talk. Even a single meaningful exchange can strengthen a relationship.
→ Research on conversation quality
🤝
Designed for every brain
Switch is built using W3C Cognitive Accessibility guidelines and inclusive design principles — making it genuinely usable for people with ADHD, autism, social anxiety, Down syndrome, dyslexia, and anyone who finds conversation hard. Design for the edges, and everyone benefits.
→ UX for neurodiversity
🫁
Calm before conversation
The built-in breathing exercise uses box breathing — a clinically validated technique for reducing anxiety in under 2 minutes. For users with social anxiety or neurodivergent conditions, having a calming tool built right in reduces the barrier to starting a conversation.
→ Tonen — neurodivergent communication tools
📵
Phone as a bridge, not a barrier
Switch is intentionally minimal — one question, then you put the phone down. No feeds, no notifications, no accounts. The app's job is done the moment you look up.
→ SpringSocial — neurodivergent social practice
💙
Inclusive for Down syndrome users
Switch follows design principles specifically validated for Down syndrome users — simple layouts, one task at a time, plain language, high contrast, large touch targets, and icon + text pairing. Many people with DS have stronger receptive (listening) skills than reading skills — which is exactly why Switch has read-aloud built in. No other conversation app has been built with this community in mind.
→ Designing for Down syndrome (Number Analytics) → Communicating with adults with Down syndrome → Down Syndrome Research Foundation — social communication
🔬
Evidence-backed question design
Questions are structured around self-disclosure — the proven driver of interpersonal closeness. Research from UCLA's PEERS program shows that structured conversational prompts lead to measurable improvements in social connection for neurodivergent adults.
→ Social skills apps & the neuroscience behind them
The vision
“Imagine a young adult with Down syndrome at work drinks. They pull out Switch, suggest a question. The group laughs, connects — and that person gets to participate more fully than they might have otherwise.”
Switch isn’t a therapy tool. It’s not a special needs app. It’s a conversation tool that works for everyone — and happens to work especially well for the people who are most often left out.
Design principles
✓ One question at a time — reduces cognitive load
✓ No time pressure — no countdowns, no timers
✓ Skip any question — full user control
✓ Plain, direct language — no jargon or idioms
✓ High contrast UI — visual accessibility
✓ Large touch targets — motor accessibility
✓ Icon + text pairing — dual-coding for comprehension
✓ Read aloud — supports dyslexia, vision needs, DS
✓ Topic filters — safe spaces for sensitive topics
✓ Breathing tool — anxiety support built in
✓ 4 font sizes — visual comfort for all users
✓ No accounts, no passwords — zero barrier to entry
✓ Works offline — anywhere, anytime
✓ Positive pacing — no pressure, no scoring
